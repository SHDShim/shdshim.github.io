Research

Science and methods for understanding planets from the inside out.

The lab’s work spans material properties, high-pressure experiments, planetary evolution, and computational tools that support interpretation.

Science

Research themes migrated from the current lab site

Planetary interiors

We study how the deep interiors of Earth, rocky planets, and volatile-rich planets evolve as a function of pressure, temperature, and composition.

Comparative planetology

Material behavior in planetary interiors is linked to broad questions in geophysics, planetary science, and astrophysics.

Materials under extreme conditions

We constrain phase relations, elasticity, and thermodynamic properties that influence planetary structure and dynamics.

Methods

Experimental and computational approaches

High-pressure experimentation

Diamond-anvil cell and related experiments probe materials at the conditions relevant to deep planetary interiors.

X-ray and synchrotron techniques

Structural and physical measurements are combined with facility-based methods to characterize materials at extreme conditions.

Modeling and data analysis

Computation, fitting, and code development connect measurements to planetary models and reproducible scientific workflows.
