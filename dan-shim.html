Principal Investigator

S.-H. Dan Shim

Mineralogist, materials scientist, planetary scientist, and geophysicist studying planetary interiors through experiments and computation.

Profile

S.-H. Dan Shim, 심상헌, 沈相憲, investigates the behavior of planetary interiors under extreme conditions through experimental and computational methods. These findings are used to model the evolution and dynamics of planets.

His work in comparative planetary science includes collaboration with seismologists, geodynamicists, planetary scientists, and astrophysicists to understand the chemical and physical evolution of planets resulting from material processes in their interiors.

Arizona State University provides a strong collaborative setting for this work through the School of Earth and Space Exploration, Materials of the Universe, and the Facility for Open Research in a Compressional Environment.

He also creates educational content using open-source computational frameworks and large language model productivity tools, with a strong interest in connecting research and education.
